# 🐪 Cairo, Egypt

**Population:** 9.6 million (metro: 21 million)  
**Language:** Arabic  
**Currency:** Egyptian Pound (E£)

---

## 🏜️ About Cairo

Cairo is the capital of Egypt and the largest city in the Arab world. Straddling the Nile River, it's a city where **ancient wonders** meet modern chaos.

> "Cairo is the mother of the world." — *Egyptian proverb*

## 🏛️ Must-See Landmarks

- **The Pyramids of Giza** — The last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World
   - Great Pyramid: built ~2560 BC for Pharaoh Khufu
   - Originally 146.6 m tall
- **The Sphinx** — Limestone statue with lion's body, human head
- **Egyptian Museum** — Over 120,000 artefacts (including Tutankhamun's mask)
- **Khan el-Khalili** — Historic bazaar (since 1382)

## 🏗️ Old vs New Cairo

| Old Cairo | New Cairo |
|-----------|-----------|
| Medieval Islamic architecture | Modern suburbs & gated communities |
| Al-Azhar Mosque (970 AD) | Cairo Festival City Mall |
| Khan el-Khalili market | New Administrative Capital |
| Narrow alleys | 6-lane highways |

## 🍽️ Egyptian Food

- **Koshari** — Rice, lentils, pasta, chickpeas with tomato sauce
- **Ful Medames** — Slow-cooked fava beans
- **Ta'amiya** (Egyptian falafel) — Made from fava beans, not chickpeas
- **Mahshi** — Stuffed vegetables (vine leaves, cabbage, peppers)
- **Basbousa** — Semolina cake soaked in syrup

## 📅 Quick Facts

1. Cairo is also called ***Al-Qahirah*** ("The Victorious")
2. The Nile flows through Cairo for about **15 km**
3. The Great Pyramid was the **tallest man-made structure** for 3,800 years
4. Cairo's metro (1987) is the **first in Africa and the Middle East**
5. The city has **over 1,000 mosques**
