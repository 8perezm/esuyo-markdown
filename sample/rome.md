# 🏛️ Rome, Italy

**Population:** 2.8 million (metro: 4.3 million)  
**Language:** Italian  
**Currency:** Euro (€)

---

## 🍝 About Rome

Rome — the *Eternal City* — is the capital of Italy. For nearly **three millennia**, it was the heart of the Roman Empire, and today it's a breathtaking blend of ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and vibrant street life.

> "Rome was not built in a day."

## 🏛️ Ancient Landmarks

| Landmark | Built | What it is |
|----------|-------|------------|
| Colosseum | 80 AD | Gladiatorial arena (50,000 seats) |
| Roman Forum | 7th C BC | Ancient civic centre |
| Pantheon | 126 AD | Best-preserved ancient building |
| Trevi Fountain | 1762 | Baroque masterpiece |
| Vatican City | 1929 | Smallest country in the world 🏳️

## 🍝 Italian Cuisine

Traditional Roman dishes you **must** try:

1. **Carbonara** — Pasta with egg, pecorino, guanciale
2. **Cacio e Pepe** — Pasta with cheese and black pepper
3. **Amatriciana** — Tomato-based sauce with guanciale
4. **Gelato** — Italian ice cream (try *stracciatella*)
5. **Tiramisù** — Coffee-flavoured layered dessert

## ⛪ Vatican City

> The Vatican is an independent city-state within Rome. It's home to:
> - **St. Peter's Basilica** — the largest church in the world
> - **Sistine Chapel** — Michelangelo's famous ceiling
> - **Vatican Museums** — 7 km of art collections

## 📅 Quick Facts

1. Rome is over **2,700 years old**
2. The Colosseum could be flooded for mock naval battles
3. Rome has **280+ fountains** and **900+ churches**
4. Modern Italians still use some Roman aqueduct water
5. The *Passetto di Borgo* is a secret passage from the Vatican to Castel Sant'Angelo
