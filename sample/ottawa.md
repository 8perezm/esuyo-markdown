# 🍁 Ottawa, Canada

**Population:** 1.1 million (metro)  
**Languages:** English & French (bilingual)  
**Currency:** Canadian Dollar (C$)

---

## 🏔️ About Ottawa

Ottawa, the capital of Canada, is a bilingual city on the Ottawa River. It's known for its **grand Victorian architecture**, world-class museums, and the magnificent **Rideau Canal** — a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

> "Ottawa is a city of museums, Parliament Hill, and a thousand conversations." — *Roy MacGregor*

## 🏛️ Must-See Landmarks

- **Parliament Hill** — Gothic Revival seat of government
   - **Peace Tower** — 92 m tall, carillon of 53 bells
   - Changing of the Guard: daily in summer
- **Rideau Canal** — 202 km waterway (freezes into **world's largest skating rink** in winter ❄️)
- **ByWard Market** — One of Canada's oldest public markets (est. 1826)
- **National Gallery of Canada** — Houses the famous *Maman* spider sculpture

## 🍽️ Canadian Food

- **Poutine** 🍟 — Fries, cheese curds, gravy (*originated in Quebec*)
- **Beavertails** — Fried dough shaped like a beaver's tail
- **Maple Syrup** 🍁 — Canada produces 70% of the world's supply
- **Butter Tarts** — Sweet pastry with buttery filling
- **Nanaimo Bars** — No-bake layered dessert bar

## 📅 Quick Facts

1. Ottawa is the **7th coldest capital** in the world
2. The city was originally called **Bytown** (named after Colonel By)
3. It became Canada's capital in **1857** (chosen by Queen Victoria)
4. The Rideau Canal was built for **military** purposes after the War of 1812
5. Ottawa has the **largest Irish** population per capita in Canada
